## What you may do without asking

- Build, install and use the software, modified or not, for yourself.
- Redistribute UNMODIFIED official source or builds, identified as such,
  with the marks intact -- e.g. mirroring a release, packaging the official
  source for a distribution that builds it unchanged (as F-Droid does).
- State facts: that your work is "based on CBG", "a fork of CBG", or
  "powered by GNU Backgammon", provided your naming and presentation do not
  suggest that Clavierhaus publishes, endorses, or supports your version.
- Keep all copyright and licence notices the GPL requires you to keep.
  Attribution required by copyright law is not trademark use.

## What requires rebranding

If you distribute a MODIFIED version of this software to others, you must
give it its own identity:

- a different application name (not "CBG", "Clavierhaus BackGammon", or
  anything confusingly similar),
- a different launcher icon (not the CBG mark or a colourable imitation),
- your own listing name and publisher identity in any store or repository,
- a different applicationId, so your builds are not mistaken for, and do not
  shadow, official ones.

You must also not sign with keys that imitate official release signing, and
you must not present your builds as official builds of Clavierhaus.

## Why

The GPL guarantees that this software stays free, forkable and independent
of us -- deliberately. The marks guarantee the opposite thing: that whatever
carries the name CBG is actually the version we built, signed and stand
behind. Both guarantees only work if they are kept apart. This is the same
arrangement long practised by Red Hat, Mozilla and the Rust project.
